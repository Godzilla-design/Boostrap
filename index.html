"5 Skincare Mistakes You're Probably Making"

Even with the best products, your skin won’t thrive if your routine has common flaws. Let’s fix that. Here are five mistakes you might be making — and how to avoid them.

1. Over-washing Your Face Washing your face more than twice a day (or using harsh cleansers) strips away natural oils, leaving your skin dry and irritated. ✔️ Tip: Use a gentle cleanser like Maddot Purify Gel Wash morning and night — nothing more.

2. Skipping Sunscreen Rain or shine, sunscreen is a must. UV damage is the leading cause of premature aging. ✔️ Tip: Apply an SPF 30+ every morning — even if you're indoors!

3. Layering Too Many Actives Mixing vitamin C, retinol, and exfoliants can irritate your skin barrier. ✔️ Tip: Introduce active ingredients one at a time. Less is more.

4. Ignoring Your Neck Your neck ages just like your face but is often forgotten in skincare routines. ✔️ Tip: Apply cleanser, serum, and moisturizer to your neck too.

5. Not Moisturizing Oily Skin Oily skin still needs hydration. Skipping moisturizer can actually trigger more oil production. ✔️ Tip: Use a lightweight, non-comedogenic moisturizer.

Pro Tip: Consistency matters more than having 10 products. Keep your routine simple, gentle, and regular.

"Morning vs Night Skincare: Why Both Routines Matter"

    🌞 Morning Routine: Protection

  • Cleanser: Wash away sweat and oil buildup from overnight.

  • Toner: Balance your skin’s pH.

  • Serum: Use antioxidants like Vitamin C to protect against environmental damage.

  • Moisturizer: Hydrate and prep the skin.

  • Sunscreen: Always finish with SPF.

Goal: Protect and prepare your skin for the day.

    🌙 Night Routine: Repair

  • Cleanser: Remove dirt, oil, and makeup from the day.

  • Exfoliate (2-3x/week): Clear dead skin cells.

  • Treatment: Apply serums with ingredients like retinol or niacinamide.

  • Moisturizer: Lock in hydration and help repair skin while you sleep.

Goal: Heal and rejuvenate overnight.

Final Thought:

Your skin works hardest at night to regenerate. Don’t skip the bedtime routine — it’s when the magic happens.
